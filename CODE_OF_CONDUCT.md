# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

The LUC-FMitF GitHub organization is maintained by members of the Loyola University Chicago Department of Computer Science research community. We are committed to fostering an open, respectful, and inclusive environment for collaboration, research, and learning.

This Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for all participants and contributors.

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and considerate in communication.
- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Provide constructive feedback.
- Accept responsibility for mistakes and learn from them.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Focus on what is best for the research community and project goals.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct.
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory language.
- Sexual harassment or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Trolling, deliberate intimidation, or sustained disruption.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Academic dishonesty, plagiarism, or misrepresentation of work.

These standards apply in all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues
- Pull requests
- Discussions
- Email communication
- Meetings or related research events

---

## Academic Integrity

As a university-affiliated research organization, contributors are expected to:

- Properly attribute ideas, code, and research contributions.
- Respect licensing requirements.
- Adhere to Loyola University Chicago’s academic integrity policies.
- Disclose conflicts of interest where appropriate.

---

## Reporting Concerns

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. Contact a repository maintainer or organization owner.
2. If necessary, escalate concerns to the Loyola University Chicago Department of Computer Science administration.

All reports will be handled respectfully and confidentially to the extent possible.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may:

- Remove inappropriate content.
- Issue warnings.
- Temporarily or permanently restrict participation.
- Escalate serious matters to university administration if required.

Enforcement decisions are at the discretion of the organization maintainers.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all contributors, maintainers, students, faculty, collaborators, and external participants interacting within this GitHub organization.

---

We are committed to maintaining a professional, inclusive, and research-focused environment.
